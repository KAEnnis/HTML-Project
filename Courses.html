Low Difficulty

Royal Dublin Golf Club

The Royal Dublin Golf Club, founded in 1885, exudes a timeless charm and rich golfing history on Bull Island. Its classic links course, designed by Harry Colt, seamlessly integrates with the natural dunescape, offering a challenging yet picturesque golfing experience. Meticulously maintained fairways and fast greens make it a favorite among golf enthusiasts. The clubhouse, a blend of tradition and modern amenities, provides a welcoming atmosphere for post-round relaxation. With stunning views of Dublin Bay and the Dublin Mountains, the Royal Dublin Golf Club combines golfing excellence with scenic beauty, making it a must-visit destination for avid golfers seeking a true Irish golfing experience.

Medium Difficulty

Luttrellstown Golf Club

Castle Golf Resort, set amidst the grandeur of a historic castle estate just outside Dublin, offers a luxurious golfing escape. The championship golf course, designed by Donald Steel, meanders through mature woodlands and around a tranquil lake, providing a challenging yet visually stunning game. Impeccable course conditions and strategic layout make it appealing to players of all skill levels. The castle itself, a 15th-century masterpiece, adds a regal backdrop to the golfing experience. With top-notch facilities, including a modern clubhouse and fine dining options, Luttrellstown Castle Golf Resort seamlessly blends golfing excellence with the charm of an aristocratic retreat.

High Difficulty

K Club

The K Club's golf course, located in County Kildare, Ireland, stands as a golfing masterpiece. Designed by Arnold Palmer, the Palmer Ryder Cup Course boasts a blend of challenging holes set against a backdrop of pristine landscapes. Majestic mature trees, the River Liffey weaving through the layout, and strategically placed bunkers create a visually stunning and demanding round. Host to the 2006 Ryder Cup, the course maintains a championship aura, attracting golf enthusiasts worldwide. The opulent K Club estate, with its historic hotel and world-class amenities, complements the golfing experience, offering a perfect synthesis of sporting challenge and luxurious indulgence.

Woodenbridge Golf Club

Woodenbridge Golf Club, nestled in the serene Vale of Avoca in County Wicklow, Ireland, showcases a golfing gem amid natural beauty. Designed by the renowned Irish architect, Joe Carr, the course unfolds along the banks of the Aughrim River, surrounded by lush woodlands and scenic landscapes. Woodenbridge's charm lies in its seamless integration with the terrain, providing a challenging yet enjoyable round for players of all levels. The course's strategic layout and well-maintained fairways contribute to its allure. The warm hospitality of the clubhouse and the tranquil surroundings make Woodenbridge Golf Club a delightful destination for those seeking a memorable golfing experience.

Low Difficulty

Portmarnock Golf Club

Portmarnock Golf Club, located on the picturesque Portmarnock Peninsula near Dublin, stands as a testament to classic links golf. Dating back to 1894, the course embodies timeless elegance and a challenging layout. Designed by William S. Flynn, its coastal setting and undulating fairways provide a true links experience. The absence of commercial distractions adds to the pure golfing atmosphere. Known for its tough par-3s and strategic bunkering, the course has hosted prestigious events, solidifying its reputation. With spectacular views of the Irish Sea and the historic clubhouse, Portmarnock Golf Club offers a traditional and exceptional golfing journey for enthusiasts and purists alike.

Medium Difficulty

The Island Golf Club

The Island Golf Club, situated on a narrow peninsula in North County Dublin, offers a captivating links golf experience. Dating back to 1890, this natural links course, redesigned by Fred Hawtree, is a harmonious blend of challenging holes set against a backdrop of rugged dunes and coastal terrain. The course's strategic layout demands precision and shot-making skill, making it a favorite among golf purists. With stunning views of Malahide Estuary and Howth Peninsula, the Island Golf Club provides a serene and scenic golfing escape. The welcoming clubhouse and rich history further enhance the overall charm of this classic Irish links course.

High Difficulty

Powerscourt Golf Club

Howth Golf Club, situated on the scenic Howth Peninsula in Dublin, Ireland, offers a delightful golfing experience. This charming parkland course, established in 1916 and designed by James Braid, meanders through undulating terrain with views of Dublin Bay and the Irish Sea. The course, surrounded by gorse and mature trees, challenges golfers with its strategic layout and well-placed hazards. The friendly atmosphere and welcoming clubhouse add to the overall appeal. Howth Golf Club provides a peaceful and enjoyable round, blending natural beauty with golfing tradition, making it a hidden gem for both local players and visitors seeking a relaxed yet rewarding golf experience.
